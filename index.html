About Me

Saman Farahmand is a Ph.D. student in the Computational Sciences program, studying Bioinformatics and Data Science tracks at the University of Massachusetts Boston. In his dissertation, he focuses on developing efficient computational methods and rigorous statistical techniques for problems at the intersection of computational, biological, and clinical sciences. He holds a master's degree in Health IT engineering from University of Tehran, Iran, that investigated network-based breast cancer biomarker discovery using game theoretical approach. Prior to starting his master, he completed his B.Sc. in IT engineering at Shahrood University of Technology at Semnan, Iran. During his academic career, he has published several papers in scientific journals and conferences centering on oncology and omics research. From 2012 to 2017, he was a data analyst at Dadeh Gostaran Asia Faranegar (DAF) Co. developing NLP and statistical learning methods in various applications including web mining and spam-filtering.

To increase the performance, we constructed a transcriptional regulatory network by integrating publicly available ChIP-seq experiments with tissue-specific RNA-seq experiments using a regularized Gaussian Graphical Model (Published in NAR 2019).

MODEX was published in JBI

Our study on using NLP to automate gene regulatory annotation,MODEX, was just published in Journal of Biomedical Informatics. Our text mining framework mines PubMed database to extract various meta data for regulatory interactions including ChIP-Seq experiments. An HTTP REST interface is also provided to make queries programmatically.
